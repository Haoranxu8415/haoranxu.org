
When application season started, I sat down alone to put together my resume. I scrolled through photos, looked through awards, laid out everything I had done over the past few years, one thing at a time.

Then I noticed something. All of it had a pattern.

At WYEF, I coordinated the art team's workflow, keeping everyone moving in the same direction under an impossible deadline. With Information Bubbles, I translated between the logic of the backend and the language of visual design. As president of the photography club, I ran workshops that brought together students at completely different skill levels. In the economics presentation, I convinced my team to take on a harder topic, then turned dense policy debates into something a room of a hundred people could actually follow.

Every time, I was standing in the middle. Turning abstract ideas into concrete action, connecting different people and resources, making things actually happen.

I had never put a single phrase to it before. But sitting there with everything laid out in front of me, I found one: bridge builder. Not the person charging ahead, not the person executing in the background, but the one in between, making communication possible.

What surprised me was that I enjoy it. Not because I had no other choice, but because that position makes me feel useful in a specific way. I can see the full picture and the details at the same time. I can understand what a creative person is trying to do and what the person executing it is actually able to do. That requires a particular kind of sensitivity, and I found that I have always had it without realising.

Writing the college essay was an interesting process for me. Not because it was easy, but because it forced me to find the most honest version of myself within a given structure. You cannot say everything, so you have to decide what actually matters. That midnight, sitting alone going through the pieces of the past few years, was when I figured it out.
