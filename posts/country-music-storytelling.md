
I listen to a lot of music. CPop, KPop, HKPop, every Taylor Swift album from her debut through Midnights. All kinds. But if I had to pick one genre, I would say country.

The reason is simple: one song, one story.

Country music has something that most pop rarely does. It is not in a hurry. It will tell you where someone lives, what they do, who they loved, what they lost. It gives you a complete slice of a life in three minutes, lets you step inside it for a while, and then lets you leave.

Taylor Swift's Mine is one of my favourite examples. The song starts with two young people meeting, and ends somewhere years later, in a kitchen, mid-argument, then reconciled. It never states its central idea directly. Instead it brings you in through detail: a boy working part-time at a restaurant, a meeting by the river, a fight, then the words "we'll be alright." The Last Time is about opening the door again after being let down too many times. All Too Well is a scarf and a relationship remembered with painful clarity. Begin Again is a person sitting alone in a cafe, slowly learning to trust again. Every song is someone standing somewhere specific, living through something specific.

I listen to music when I am moving. Walking, on the bus, any moment in transit. I have noticed that different music gives the same journey a completely different feeling, the beat and the landscape weaving into something that is hard to describe. A good country song paired with the right stretch of road can make me feel like I am not just listening to someone else's story. I am living it for a moment.

I have always thought that one of the most remarkable things humans do is tell stories. Not because stories help us escape reality, but because good ones pull us deeper into it, into someone else's reality, into a feeling we would never have felt otherwise, into a version of the world we would never have seen on our own.

That is what country music gives me. And it is what I have always wanted to do.
