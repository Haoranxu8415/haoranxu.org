
In June 2023, the high school entrance exams ended. I did not know what to do with the silence that followed, so I decided to pick up a hobby. I chose photography.

The reasons were simple, almost embarrassingly so: I wanted a way to express myself, and I needed some good desktop wallpapers.

That was how it started.

I am not the kind of photographer who stands in one place for an hour waiting for the light to change. I like to move. The frame is never planned in advance, it is something I stumble into while walking. When the viewfinder suddenly shows me something and I think, that is it, the feeling that follows is difficult to replicate with anything else.

But I rarely photograph people.

Not because people do not interest me. It is actually the opposite. I photograph for people, I just do not put them in the frame. What I want to give someone looking at my photos is an atmosphere, an environment, a place I have been and loved. I want them to sit on the other side of the screen and walk into that space, to feel what I felt when I was there.

There are no people in the frame. But the subject of the experience has always been a person. Just not me. You.

This is also why I do not think of photography as documentation. Documentation fixes a moment in place, for yourself. What I am trying to do is extend an invitation: to take something I lived through and hand it to someone I will probably never meet, so they can live through it too.

For me, photography has never been about technique. It is a language.
