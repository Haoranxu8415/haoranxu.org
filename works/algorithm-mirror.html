Design · Web

Information Bubbles

An interactive web-based prototype that simulates the inner workings of social media recommendation algorithms. Users swipe through short-form video content, with their interactions — likes, skips, dwell time, interest selections — logged and analysed in real time.

Based on this behaviour, the system generates a personalised summary at session end, visualising the algorithm's "understanding" of the user's preferences. The project explores how seemingly neutral data tracking can influence self-perception, highlighting the psychological and ethical dimensions of algorithmic personalisation.

Built with HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript; designed with a minimalist aesthetic aiming to be both elegant and immersive.
