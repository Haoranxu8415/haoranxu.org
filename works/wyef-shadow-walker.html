Business · Design

WYEF · Shadow Walker

WYEF is an international business simulation competition. Our brief was to design a complete commercial proposal for a fictional game studio. From brief to submission, the timeline was brutal.

My role covered the visual concept design for the entire project: the game's world, its visual language, its brand identity. I also coordinated the art team's workflow. With a deadline that left almost no room, the question was not just what to make, but how to divide the work so that everyone could move fast without the quality falling apart. We aligned on visual direction and style standards early, which meant the later stages could actually run smoothly.

The biggest problem came in post-production. We recorded a human voiceover for the pitch video, and hit a wall: severe background noise that conventional tools could not fix. I made the call to drop the human voice entirely and use AI voiceover instead. This was 2024, when these tools were still far from mature. The result was unexpectedly professional. One judge described the video as fresh and noted that it communicated a complex concept clearly within a tight runtime. I think part of that came from a voice that did not sound like a typical student project.

We received the Best Narrative Award and finished Global Bronze, placing in the top 20 of 76 teams.
